Field of Interest: Physical Organic Chemistry

What is Physical Organic Chemistry?

Physical organic chemistry is a branch of organic chemistry that focuses less on synthesis in a laboratory and more on how the reactions take place and probing potential mechanisms. It applies the experimental nature of physical chemistry to the analysis of organic molecules. It tends to focus more on examining the reactivity of molecules and theorizing about transition states than actual wet chemistry synthesis.

My interest lies specifically in the computational side of the field, which focuses more on examining reactions and energy changes through simulations and molecular modeling using both Spartan and Gaussian software packages. When I do start my graduate research, I would like to go in the direction of using quantum mechanical calculations to study reactions and mechanisms.
